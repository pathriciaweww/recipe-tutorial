Berry Panna Cotta

Description

The recipes have been selected because they present low-carb, high-fat options that anyone can use. If you are tracking your macros carefully, it would be helpful to create meal plans using these and other recipes so that you can monitor your daily intake. You'll find plenty of tools online to help support you in this too.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup strawberries, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup raspberries, roughly chopped + whole berries, to garnish
  • 2 tsps lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp + 2 tsps gelatin powder
  • 1 cup thickened cream
  • 50g erythritol
  • 2 tbsps cold water
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence

Steps

  1. Place strawberries, raspberries, lemon juice and 2 tablespoons of erythritol in a saucepan over mdeium heat, stir until just boiling. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes. Remove from heat. Press the cooled mixture through a sieve and discard the solids.
  2. Combine 2 teaspoons of gelatin with 2 tablespoons cold water and stir this into the berry mixture. Divide in half. Set one half aside in a warm place. Spoon equal parts of the other half between the serving glasses. Transfer to the fridge while making the panna cotta.
  3. Place cream, milk, remaining erythritol and vanilla essence in a saucepan over medium heat and whisk until simmering. Remove from heat and allow to cool. Combine remaining gelatin powder with the 1/4 cup of water. Stir into the cooled panna cotta mixture. Cool for a further 15 minutes then pour on top of the berry mixture over the top of the panna cotta. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, before serving.
