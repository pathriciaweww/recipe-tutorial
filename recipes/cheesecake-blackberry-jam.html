Cheesecake with Blackberry Jam

Description

The recipes have been selected because they present low-carb, high-fat options that anyone can use. If you are tracking your macros carefully, it would be helpful to create meal plans using these and other recipes so that you can monitor your daily intake. You'll find plenty of tools online to help support you in this too.

Ingredients

  • 700g cream cheese, room temperature
  • 140g erythritol
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 185ml sour cream
  • 1 lemon, to make 1tbsp lemon zest + 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 5 cups blackberries, fresh or frozen
  • 75g butter softened
  • 2 tsps vanilla escence
  • Pinch of stevia powder

Steps

  1. To make the cheesecake, preheat the oven to 150C and grease and line a 22cm springform tin with baking paper.
  2. Place the cream cheese and butter in a mixer and beat until smooth. Mix in the erythritol, then the eggs, one at a time. Miz through the sour cream, 1 teaspoon of the lemon zest and 1 teaspoon of the lemon juice and the vanilla essence.
  3. Pour the mixture into the cake tin and smooth the top into an even layer. Bake for 90 minutes until pale yellow on top and a skewer inserted into the middle comes out clean. Let cool for at least 2 hours.
  4. Meanwhile, to make the jam, place all the remaining ingredients into a saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce to a low simmer and coook for at least 30 minutes, until thickened and reduced by nearly half. Stir frequently and break up the berries. Add more stevia to taste, if need, and remove from heat.
  5. Let cool to room temperature and serve drizzled over the cake.
