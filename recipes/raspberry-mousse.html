Raspberry Mousse

Description

The recipes have been selected because they present low-carb, high-fat options that anyone can use. If you are tracking your macros carefully, it would be helpful to create meal plans using these and other recipes so that you can monitor your daily intake. You'll find plenty of tools online to help support you in this too.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (250g, 8oz) fresh or frozen raspberries + extra for garnish
  • 1½ cups (375ml, 13fl oz) thickened cream
  • ½ cup (125g, 4oz) creamed ricotta
  • 3 egg whites
  • ½ tsp liquid stevia

Steps

  1. Heat the raspberries in a small saucepan over medium heat for 10 minutes until they have disintegrated. Press the pulp through a sieve to remove seeds. Discard the seeds.
  2. Whisk the cream until stiff peaks form, then fold the ricotta and raspberry pulp through it until combines.
  3. Whisk the egg whites and stevia together until stiff peaks form.
  4. Fold the raspberry mixture through the egg whites until combined.
  5. Divide between serving dishes and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
  6. Serve garnished with fresh raspberries.
